Inspiration

Last year I was presenting The Paradise, but now, after a year, so many things have happened to me. And now London has become my 'Burning Heaven'. I have put all my feelings for the city into this drink, the sweet and sour happiness, and the slow burning pain. All of this makes a fantastically warm winterish taste drink, because the weather in London, most of the time, is as cold as my ex's heart.

Ingredients

  • 50 ml - Mr Black

  • 3 dash - chilli

  • 15 ml - sugarsyrup

  • 3 g - English Breakfast

  • 25 g - coffee

  • Blood of a virgin (optional)

The recipe

This drink starts with a tea-coffee infusion made with a syphon, using a vanilla flavoured English breakfast tea, and for the coffee I chose a washed process Burundi, which gives a nice toffee flavour. (Any coffee with a toffee, chocolate, or caramel flavour can be used.) I put the tea into the bottom chamber, filled it up with hot water, lit the burner and waited. Once the water moved to the top vessel, I added the coffee, left it for a minute, then turned off the heat and waited until the coffee went back to the tea.

At the same time, I put some ice into a shaker, along with some Mr Black coffee Liqueur, sugar syrup, and a few dashs of chili sauce. I gave it a good shake, and double strained it into a shot glass which had a sugar decoration on it.

When the tea-coffee infusion is ready, I strained it over the cold ingredients, and it created a nice warm drink that you can drink immediately. Then the choice is yours, you can drink this as shot, or just drink it slowly and gently.
